# vs-ai2.47: Decline negative capture exchanges (exchange-ratio blindness)

## Overview

The bot takes negative-ratio capture exchanges: during an attack it captures
(fortifies) one enemy cell while allowing the opponent to capture two of its own
in reply — a 1-for-2 that loses material. Owner heuristic: "if during an attack I
fortify one cell and allow the opponent to fortify two of mine, that move is
probably bad." Fix the static evaluation so a 1-for-2 nets negative, while a
genuine 2-for-1 stays taken.

**Game semantics (load-bearing):** a Move onto an enemy **Normal** cell adjacent
to your **connected** territory CAPTURES it, converting it to your permanent
**Fortified** cell (uncapturable thereafter). Your own Normal cells adjacent to an
opponent's connected territory are capturable by that opponent next turn. An
"exchange" at contact is a permanent cell-conversion trade.

## Grounded root cause (already measured — do NOT re-investigate)

- **Anchor game 91ff20fd** (owner LuckyEagle12 beats Bot 4125, 12x12):
  reconstructs exactly to P2 elimination (winner P1, final owned 18 vs 10).
  - Turn 6: bot net +1 cells / human net +2 — a 1-for-2.
  - Turn 8: bot net +1 / human net +2 — another 1-for-2.
- **Depth does NOT cure it:** at search depth 10, turn 6, the current post-TT
  engine (base 3ff6d50, includes vs-ai2.41 TT rework) still scores the
  pre-exchange position POSITIVE (+1971). Production reaches only ~depth 6-8 in
  these mid-game positions (depth-10 = 2.3M-15.6M nodes). **The fix must be a
  STATIC eval correction, not a depth bet.**
- **Why a 1-for-2 reads positive** (evaluate.go ~199-238): capturing an enemy
  Normal gains fortified (weight 6 ≈ +42) + connected (+69) on my side AND removes
  an enemy Normal (weight 30 ≈ -208) + connected (-69); via the 2-player
  differential utility that is ~+388 to my utility per capture. The retaliation it
  enables (2 of my Normals now capturable next turn) is priced ONLY by the static
  `threatened`/`threatenedLoss` terms (lines 208-209:
  `threatTempo*ratio(threatened, connected)`), which are ratio-normalized
  (÷connected) and small versus the ~277/cell material swing the opponent realizes.
  The +150 predatory bonus (line 234) rewards being adjacent to enemy cut cells
  with no symmetric penalty for my own cells in the same danger. **Gain priced at
  full material, enabled loss priced at a small proxy → 1-for-2 nets positive.**

## Technical Details

- Utility model (2-player): `utility[me] = raw[me] - raw[opp]`. A per-capture
  material swing is ~+388 to my utility; a retaliated capture by the opponent is
  a comparable swing the static eval must anticipate.
- The new term should be roughly: for each own connected Normal capturable by an
  opponent next turn, subtract a material-scale penalty (comparable to the
  ~208-277 a lost Normal is worth in the differential), so N exposed cells net
  against the gain. Keep it bounded/normalized to avoid dominating the material
  terms in non-contact positions.
- Determinism: no wall-clock; use node-budget/depth searches in gates.
